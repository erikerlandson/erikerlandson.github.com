Computing Smooth Max and its Gradients Without Over- and Underflow

May 28th, 2018 | Feedback

In my previous post I derived the gradient and Hessian for the smooth max function. The Notorious JDC wrote a helpful companion post that describes computational issues of overflow and underflow with smooth max; values of fk don’t have to grow very large (or small) before floating point limitations start to force their exponentials to +inf or zero. In JDC’s post he discusses this topic in terms of only two-valued smooth max. However it isn’t hard to generalize the idea to a collection of fk.
